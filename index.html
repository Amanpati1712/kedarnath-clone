Kedarnath is a town located in the Rudraprayag district of the state of Uttarakhand, India. It is situated in the Himalayas at an altitude of approximately 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level. Kedarnath is famous for its ancient and sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as the Kedarnath Temple.

Information

The town is most famous for the ancient Kedarnath Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is believed to have been built by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (divine manifestations of Lord Shiva) in India. The temple attracts a large number of devotees and pilgrims each year, especially during the summer months when it is accessible.

Kedarnath is located in the midst of scenic natural surroundings, surrounded by the majestic peaks of the Garhwal Himalayas. It is situated near the head of the Mandakini River, a tributary of the Alaknanda River. The area offers breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains, lush meadows, and pristine valleys, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and trekking enthusiasts.

In 2013, Kedarnath faced a devastating natural disaster when heavy rainfall and floods caused massive landslides in the region. The disaster resulted in significant damage to the town and the temple, leading to the loss of many lives. Since then, efforts have been made to rebuild and restore the area, and it has gradually regained its popularity as a pilgrimage and tourist destination.
